Australian Road Accident Report

Australian residents own an average of 1.8 cars per census household, and cars remain the most popular transport to work in every state and territory. Therefore, the issue of road safety is of great concern. In Australia, about 14,000 traffic accidents were counted between 2010 and 2021. NSW is the state with the most road accidents with 4,081.

NSW recorded the most road accidents in 2010 with 403 times. Since then the decline has remained at over 300 road accidents per year. Until it falls below 300 per year from 2020. Taken as a whole, the number of road crashes in every Australian state has been on a downward trend. This means that more and more people are starting to pay attention to road safety.

The age group with the most road accidents is the 40 to 64, most of whom are in the final stages of their careers and are preparing to retire. Therefore, people in this age group need to pay more attention to road safety.

The age of most road accidents is 19 years old with a total of 375, which is a phenomenon that needs attention. Because this age is when many people have just obtained their driver's licenses, they need to pay more attention to their own protection and driving safety.

The day with the most road accidents was Saturday with a total of 2,351. At the same time, both Friday and Sunday exceeded 2,000 times. This reminds everyone to pay attention to traffic safety on weekends and Fridays. Don't relax your vigilance about driving safety because of the holiday.
